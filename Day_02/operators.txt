-> Operatorsare action words of programming.
-> They tell Python what to do with variables and their values.
-> There are six Types of operators

1. Arithmetic Operators:
    Addition (+)
    Subtraction (-)
    Multiplication (*)
    Division (/)
    Floor division (//)
    Modulus (%)
    Exponent (**)

2. Comparison Operators
    Equal To (==)
    Not Equal To (!=)
    Less Than (<)
    Greater Than (>)
    Less Than Equal To (<=)
    Greater Than Equal To (>=)

3. Logical Operators
    Both Must Be True (AND)
    At Least One True (OR)
    Flips True/Flase (NOT)

4. Assignment Operators
    Assign (=)
    Add & Assign (+=)
    Subtract & Assign(-=)
    Multiply & Assign(*=)
    Divide & Assign(/=)
    Floor Division & Assign(//=)
    Modulus & Assign(%=)
    Exponent & Assign(**=)

5. Identity & Membership Operators
    is      --> Identity Operator
    is not  --> Identity Operator
    in      --> Membership Operator
    not in  --> Membership Operator

6. Bitwise Operators
    AND (&)
    OR (|)
    XOR (^)
    NOT (~)
    Left Shift (<<)     --> For LS xply a no by power of 2
    Right Shift (>>)    --> For RS Floor Div a no by power of 2

Precedence Of Operators
()  ->  **  ->  *  /  //  %   ->    +  -   ->   comparison operators   ->   not   ->    and   ->    or
